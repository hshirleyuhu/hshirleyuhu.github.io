This is what New Yorkers are complaining about?

By Xuechen Shirley Hu

Published July 29, 2019

3-1-1, a number New Yorkers call or text whenever they are looking for call for a city service.

This project uses the 3-1-1 data of the the past six months to see what are the top issues New Yorkers concerned about.

Without any surprise, urgent service requires like heat outage ranks on the top of the list, which followed by traffic concerns.

Sources: The New York Times

When breaked down by month, unsanitary conditions are reported to 3-1-1 more often in winter than summer.

(Unsanitary complaints by month)

The subcategories under unsanitary conditions are pests, garbage storage, mold and pests which are mainly known as summer evils.

Residents lives on heat in winter. But it seems that they are not the only creature take advantage of the warmth. The major concern in winter regarding unsanitary conditions for New Yorkers is pests according to the 3-1-1 records. Pests get indoor to survive the freezing weather of the season.

Garbage storage, without any surprise, gain more attention as the weather get warmer and the odor become unavoidable.

Other complaints correlated with weather are noises. Noise complaints in residential buildings raise in summer and reach their peak in June.

Sources: NYC Open Data
